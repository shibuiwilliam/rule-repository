MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT — KEY CLAUSES (EXCERPT)

Effective Date: March 1, 2025
Parties: Acme Solutions Inc. ("Provider") and GlobalTech Corp. ("Client")

--- SECTION 5: LIMITATION OF LIABILITY ---

5.1 EXCEPT FOR OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTIONS 7 (INDEMNIFICATION), 8
(CONFIDENTIALITY), AND CLAIMS ARISING FROM WILLFUL MISCONDUCT OR GROSS
NEGLIGENCE, NEITHER PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT
SHALL EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT IN THE
TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM, OR (B) FIVE
HUNDRED THOUSAND U.S. DOLLARS ($500,000).

5.2 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA,
OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION OR THEORY OF
LIABILITY.

--- SECTION 6: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ---

6.1 Pre-Existing IP. Each Party retains all rights in its pre-existing
intellectual property. Neither Party acquires any rights in the other Party's
pre-existing IP except as expressly granted herein.

6.2 Deliverables. Upon full payment, Client shall own all intellectual
property rights in custom deliverables created specifically for Client under
a SOW. Provider retains ownership of its tools, methodologies, and
pre-existing components incorporated into deliverables, and grants Client
an irrevocable, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such components as
part of the deliverables.

6.3 Jointly Developed IP. Any intellectual property jointly developed by the
Parties shall be jointly owned. Each Party may use jointly developed IP without
accounting to the other, but neither Party may license it to third parties
without the other Party's written consent.

--- SECTION 7: INDEMNIFICATION ---

7.1 Mutual Indemnification. Each Party ("Indemnifying Party") shall defend,
indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party from and against any third-party
claims, damages, and reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) arising
from: (a) the Indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement; (b) the
Indemnifying Party's negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) infringement
of third-party intellectual property rights by the Indemnifying Party's
materials or deliverables.

7.2 Procedure. The indemnified Party shall: (a) provide prompt written notice
of any claim; (b) grant the Indemnifying Party sole control of the defense
and settlement; and (c) provide reasonable cooperation at the Indemnifying
Party's expense.

--- SECTION 9: TERMINATION ---

9.1 For Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty
(30) days' written notice. Client shall pay for all work completed and
non-cancelable commitments incurred prior to the effective date of
termination.

9.2 For Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon material breach
if the breaching Party fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written
notice specifying the breach. Either Party may terminate immediately if the
other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or makes an assignment
for the benefit of creditors.

--- SECTION 11: GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION ---

11.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the
State of Delaware, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles.

11.2 Dispute Resolution. The Parties shall attempt to resolve disputes
through good-faith negotiation for thirty (30) days. If unresolved, disputes
shall be submitted to binding arbitration under the rules of the American
Arbitration Association in Wilmington, Delaware, before a single arbitrator.

--- SECTION 12: DATA PROTECTION ---

12.1 Each Party shall comply with all applicable data protection laws,
including GDPR and CCPA, in connection with any personal data processed
under this Agreement.

12.2 If Provider processes personal data on behalf of Client, the Parties
shall execute a Data Processing Agreement in substantially the form attached
as Exhibit B.

12.3 Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational
security measures and shall notify the other Party of any personal data
breach within seventy-two (72) hours of discovery.

--- SECTION 13: FORCE MAJEURE ---

13.1 Neither Party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance caused
by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters,
pandemics, government actions, war, terrorism, or widespread infrastructure
failures, provided the affected Party gives prompt written notice and uses
reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

13.2 If a force majeure event continues for more than ninety (90) days,
either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice.
