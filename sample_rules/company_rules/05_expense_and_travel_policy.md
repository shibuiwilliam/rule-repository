# Expense and Travel Policy

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** POL-015  
**Version:** 1.5  
**Effective Date:** 2025-01-01  
**Owner:** Finance Department  
**Approved by:** CFO  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs business travel arrangements and expense reimbursement for YourExampleCompany employees engaged in client consultations, conferences, and business development activities.

---

## 2. Pre-Approval Requirements

- MUST obtain manager approval before booking any business travel.
- MUST obtain VP-level approval for international travel.
- MUST obtain CFO approval for expenses exceeding $5,000 per trip.
- SHOULD submit travel requests at least 10 business days in advance.
- MUST NOT book non-refundable arrangements without pre-approval.

---

## 3. Travel Booking

### 3.1 Air Travel
- MUST book economy class for flights under 5 hours.
- MAY book premium economy for flights between 5-8 hours.
- MAY book business class for flights over 8 hours with VP approval.
- MUST book through the company's approved travel platform (SAP Concur).
- SHOULD book at least 14 days in advance for best rates.

### 3.2 Accommodation
- MUST stay at hotels within the company's per-night budget: $200 (domestic), $300 (international).
- SHOULD prefer partner hotels listed in the approved vendor catalog.
- MAY exceed the budget limit with manager approval for high-cost cities (NYC, SF, London, Tokyo).
- MUST NOT book luxury or 5-star accommodations without VP approval.

### 3.3 Ground Transportation
- SHOULD use public transportation where practical and safe.
- MAY use ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft) for business purposes.
- MUST NOT rent luxury or sports vehicles.
- MUST document the business purpose for all ground transportation expenses.

---

## 4. Client Entertainment

- MAY expense meals with clients up to $75 per person.
- MUST NOT expense alcohol exceeding 2 drinks per person per meal.
- MUST include the client's name, company, and business purpose on the receipt.
- MUST obtain manager approval for entertainment expenses exceeding $200.
- MUST NOT expense entertainment that could be considered inappropriate or offensive.

---

## 5. Daily Allowances

| Category | Domestic | International |
|---|---|---|
| Meals (no receipt required) | $60/day | $80/day |
| Incidentals | $20/day | $30/day |
| Internet/communications | $15/day | $25/day |

- MAY claim per diem for meals instead of individual receipts.
- MUST NOT claim per diem AND submit individual meal receipts for the same day.

---

## 6. Expense Submission

### 6.1 Timeline
- MUST submit expense reports within 14 days of trip completion.
- MUST submit reports within 30 days of the expense date for non-travel items.
- Expenses submitted after 60 days will NOT be reimbursed without CFO exception.

### 6.2 Documentation
- MUST attach original receipts for all expenses over $25.
- MUST include business purpose, project code, and client name for all expenses.
- MUST itemize expenses — grouped receipts will be rejected.
- MUST convert foreign currency expenses to USD at the date-of-transaction rate.

### 6.3 Reimbursement
- Approved expenses are reimbursed within 10 business days of submission.
- Corporate card expenses are auto-reconciled — MUST still submit receipts and categorize.

---

## 7. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are NOT reimbursable:
- Personal entertainment or leisure activities
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, or legal fines
- Upgrades beyond policy limits without pre-approval
- Expenses for family members or non-business companions
- Personal clothing, toiletries, or grooming
- Gym memberships during travel (unless hotel-provided)
- Loss or theft of personal belongings

---

## 8. Conference and Training

- MAY attend up to 2 conferences per year with manager approval.
- MUST document the learning objectives and expected value before booking.
- MUST share key learnings with the team within 2 weeks of attendance.
- MUST NOT exceed $3,000 per conference (including registration, travel, and accommodation).

---

## 9. Compliance

- MUST NOT submit fraudulent or inflated expense claims.
- Fraudulent claims will result in immediate termination and potential legal action.
- Finance may audit any expense report — MUST cooperate with documentation requests.
- Repeated policy violations may result in loss of corporate card privileges.
