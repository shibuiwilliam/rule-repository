---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the expectations, responsibilities, and guidelines for employees working remotely. YourExampleCompany operates a hybrid-first model, enabling flexibility while maintaining productivity and collaboration.

---

## 2. Eligibility

- All employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period are eligible for remote work.
- Remote work arrangements must be agreed upon with the direct manager.
- Certain roles may require on-site presence for client engagements.

---

## 3. Working Hours and Availability

### 3.1 Core Hours
- MUST be available during core business hours: 10:00–16:00 in the employee's local timezone.
- MUST communicate working schedule to the team via the shared calendar.
- MAY adjust start/end times flexibly outside core hours with manager agreement.

### 3.2 Responsiveness
- MUST respond to Slack messages within 30 minutes during working hours.
- MUST respond to emails within 4 business hours.
- MUST join scheduled meetings on time (within 2 minutes of start).
- SHOULD indicate availability status (online/busy/away) in Slack at all times.

### 3.3 Time Tracking
- MUST log working hours accurately in the time tracking system daily.
- MUST log client-billable hours separately with project codes.
- MUST NOT exceed 45 hours per week without prior management approval.
- MUST take a minimum 30-minute break for every 6 consecutive hours worked.

---

## 4. Workspace Requirements

### 4.1 Physical Environment
- MUST maintain a dedicated, quiet workspace suitable for video calls and focused work.
- MUST have a stable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload).
- SHOULD use an external monitor, keyboard, and ergonomic chair for daily work.
- MUST ensure the workspace is free from distractions during client meetings.

### 4.2 Security Requirements
- MUST follow the Information Security Policy (POL-005) at all times when working remotely.
- MUST use the company VPN when accessing internal systems.
- MUST NOT work on client-sensitive materials in public spaces (cafes, coworking spaces) without a privacy screen.
- MUST lock the workstation when stepping away, even at home.

---

## 5. Communication and Collaboration

### 5.1 Meetings
- MUST enable video for all client-facing meetings unless bandwidth issues prevent it.
- SHOULD enable video for internal team meetings.
- MUST use professional backgrounds (real or virtual) for client meetings.
- MUST mute when not speaking in meetings with 4+ participants.

### 5.2 Asynchronous Communication
- SHOULD default to asynchronous communication for non-urgent matters.
- MUST document decisions from synchronous discussions in the appropriate channel or wiki.
- SHOULD use threaded conversations in Slack to keep channels organized.
- MUST NOT expect immediate responses outside core hours.

---

## 6. Equipment and Expenses

- The company provides a laptop, monitor, and necessary peripherals for home office setup.
- MAY claim up to $100/month for internet and co-working space expenses.
- MUST return all company equipment within 7 days of employment termination.
- MUST report any damaged or malfunctioning equipment within 24 hours.

---

## 7. Health and Well-being

- MUST take regular breaks (recommended: 5 minutes every hour).
- SHOULD use the company-provided mental health support resources when needed.
- MUST NOT be expected to work while on approved sick leave.
- SHOULD participate in virtual team social events to maintain team cohesion.

---

## 8. Performance

- Performance is measured by output and deliverables, not hours logged.
- MUST participate in regular 1:1 meetings with direct manager (minimum bi-weekly).
- MUST complete all assigned deliverables by agreed deadlines.
- Repeated failure to meet expectations may result in remote work privileges being revoked.
