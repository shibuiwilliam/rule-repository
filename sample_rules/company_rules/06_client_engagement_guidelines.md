## 1. Purpose

This document defines the standards for client engagement across the lifecycle of consultation projects — from pre-sales through delivery to handover. It ensures consistent, high-quality service delivery.

---

## 2. Pre-Engagement

### 2.1 Scoping
- MUST conduct a formal discovery session before providing estimates.
- MUST document assumptions, constraints, and dependencies in the Statement of Work (SOW).
- MUST NOT commit to timelines without consulting the technical team lead.
- SHOULD provide a range estimate (optimistic/expected/pessimistic) rather than a single number.
- MUST include a risk register in all proposals exceeding $50,000.

### 2.2 Contracting
- MUST use the company-approved SOW and MSA templates.
- MUST have legal review for contracts exceeding $100,000 or 6 months duration.
- MUST NOT agree to unlimited liability clauses without VP and legal approval.
- MUST define change management procedures in the contract.
- MUST include data handling and NDA provisions for all engagements.

---

## 3. Project Kickoff

### 3.1 Team Assignment
- MUST assign a dedicated project lead for all engagements exceeding 2 weeks.
- MUST NOT assign consultants to more than 2 active client projects simultaneously.
- MUST communicate team changes to the client at least 5 business days in advance.
- SHOULD maintain a 1:8 consultant-to-project-lead ratio maximum.

### 3.2 Kickoff Activities
- MUST conduct a formal kickoff meeting within the first week of engagement.
- MUST establish communication channels, cadence, and escalation paths.
- MUST document and distribute the project charter to all stakeholders.
- MUST agree on definition of done (DoD) for all deliverables.

---

## 4. Communication Standards

### 4.1 Regular Reporting
- MUST provide written status reports to the client weekly (or per agreed cadence).
- MUST include: progress vs plan, risks, blockers, upcoming milestones, and resource updates.
- MUST escalate blockers to the client within 24 hours of identification.
- MUST NOT surprise the client — communicate risks early, even if uncertain.

### 4.2 Meetings
- MUST send agendas at least 24 hours before scheduled meetings.
- MUST distribute meeting minutes within 24 hours of the meeting.
- MUST track action items with owners and due dates.
- SHOULD limit meetings to 50 minutes to allow buffer between calls.

### 4.3 Escalation Path
- Level 1: Consultant → Client counterpart (resolve within 2 business days)
- Level 2: Project Lead → Client project manager (resolve within 3 business days)
- Level 3: Account Director → Client sponsor (resolve within 5 business days)
- MUST escalate if resolution exceeds the timeframe at each level.

---

## 5. Delivery Standards

### 5.1 Quality Assurance
- MUST follow the Software Development Standards (STD-001) for all technical deliverables.
- MUST conduct internal peer reviews before client-facing deliverables.
- MUST perform a quality gate review at each milestone.
- MUST NOT deliver work that has not been tested in a staging-equivalent environment.

### 5.2 Change Management
- MUST document all scope changes as Change Requests (CRs).
- MUST NOT begin work on scope changes without written client approval.
- MUST update timelines and budgets when CRs are approved.
- SHOULD present CR impact assessment within 3 business days of request.

### 5.3 Risk Management
- MUST maintain an active risk register throughout the engagement.
- MUST review risks in weekly status meetings.
- MUST develop mitigation plans for all risks with probability > 30% or impact > Medium.
- MUST NOT accept risks that jeopardize the client's production systems without explicit sign-off.

---

## 6. Knowledge Transfer and Handover

### 6.1 Documentation Requirements
- MUST deliver: architecture documentation, operational runbooks, API documentation, and data dictionary.
- MUST conduct at least 3 knowledge transfer sessions with the client's maintenance team.
- MUST provide recorded walkthroughs of complex system components.
- SHOULD pair with client developers for at least 1 week during transition.

### 6.2 Post-Engagement Support
- MUST provide a 2-week hypercare period after go-live or handover.
- MUST document known issues and workarounds in a living document.
- SHOULD offer an optional retainer for ongoing advisory support.
- MUST conduct a post-project retrospective with the client within 2 weeks of completion.

---

## 7. Client Satisfaction

### 7.1 Feedback Collection
- MUST conduct a client satisfaction survey at project completion.
- SHOULD conduct pulse checks at monthly intervals for engagements > 3 months.
- MUST act on feedback scoring below 4.0/5.0 with a corrective action plan within 5 business days.

### 7.2 Relationship Management
- MUST maintain relationship continuity — avoid frequent team rotations.
- SHOULD schedule quarterly business reviews for ongoing accounts.
- MUST NOT use client references without explicit written permission.

---

## 8. Billing and Invoicing

- MUST submit accurate timesheets weekly by end-of-day Friday.
- MUST classify time correctly: billable, non-billable (internal), or investment (pre-sales).
- MUST NOT bill the client for internal meetings, training, or onboarding time unless contracted.
- MUST align billing with the agreed rate card and SOW terms.
- MUST report billing discrepancies within 5 business days of discovery.
