# Leave Policy

Comprehensive leave entitlements for all employees.

## Annual Paid Leave

Employees earn annual paid leave based on continuous service:
- 6 months: 10 days
- 1.5 years: 11 days
- 2.5 years: 12 days
- 3.5 years: 14 days
- 4.5 years: 16 days
- 5.5 years: 18 days
- 6.5+ years: 20 days (maximum)

### Mandatory Usage
Employees with 10 or more days of entitlement must take at least 5 days per year. The employer must proactively manage this requirement.

### Carryover
Unused leave may be carried over for up to 2 years. Leave older than 2 years expires.

### Half-Day Leave
Half-day leave may be taken up to 5 times per year (morning or afternoon).

## Special Leave (Paid)

| Type | Days |
|------|------|
| Marriage (self) | 5 days |
| Marriage (child) | 2 days |
| Bereavement (spouse/parent/child) | 5 days |
| Bereavement (grandparent/sibling) | 3 days |
| Bereavement (in-law parent) | 3 days |
| Bereavement (other relative) | 1 day |
| Birth of child (father) | 2 days |
| Natural disaster affecting residence | As needed |

## Maternity and Childcare

- Prenatal leave: 6 weeks before expected date (14 weeks for multiple pregnancy)
- Postnatal leave: 8 weeks after delivery (mandatory)
- Childcare leave: Up to child's 1st birthday (extendable to 2 years if daycare unavailable)
- Paternity leave at birth: Up to 4 weeks within 8 weeks of birth

## Family Care Leave

Up to 93 days per eligible family member, divisible into up to 3 periods.

## Sick Leave

The company provides 5 days of paid sick leave per year, separate from annual paid leave. Extended illness beyond 5 days requires use of annual paid leave or unpaid leave with medical certification.
