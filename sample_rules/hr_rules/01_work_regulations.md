# Work Regulations (就業規則)

Standard work regulations template for Japanese corporations with 10+ employees.

## Chapter 1: General Provisions

### Article 1 (Purpose)
These work regulations establish the working conditions and standards of conduct for all employees of the Company, in accordance with the Labor Standards Act and related laws.

### Article 2 (Scope)
These regulations apply to all regular employees. Part-time and contract employees are governed by separate regulations, with these regulations applying supplementarily for matters not addressed.

## Chapter 2: Working Hours and Breaks

### Article 3 (Working Hours)
Regular working hours are 8 hours per day and 40 hours per week, excluding break time. Starting time is 9:00 and ending time is 18:00.

### Article 4 (Break Time)
A 60-minute break is granted from 12:00 to 13:00. Employees working more than 6 hours receive at least 45 minutes of break; those working more than 8 hours receive at least 60 minutes.

### Article 5 (Overtime and Holiday Work)
The Company may require employees to work beyond regular hours or on holidays pursuant to a 36 Agreement filed with the Labor Standards Inspection Office. Overtime premium pay is 25% above base wage (50% for monthly overtime exceeding 60 hours).

### Article 6 (Late-Night Work)
Work between 22:00 and 05:00 carries a 25% premium. Employees under 18 shall not be assigned late-night work.

## Chapter 3: Holidays and Leave

### Article 7 (Holidays)
Employees receive Saturdays, Sundays, national holidays, year-end/new-year holidays (December 29 - January 3), and company-designated holidays.

### Article 8 (Annual Paid Leave)
Employees who have worked continuously for 6 months with at least 80% attendance are entitled to 10 days of annual paid leave, increasing with tenure to a maximum of 20 days. Employees with 10+ days of entitlement must take at least 5 days per year.

### Article 9 (Special Leave)
Employees are entitled to the following special leaves: marriage (5 days), bereavement (1-5 days depending on relationship), maternity (as per law), childcare leave, and family care leave.
