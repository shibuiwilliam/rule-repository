# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Guidelines

## Purpose

These guidelines establish the Company's obligations for detecting, preventing, and reporting potential money laundering and terrorist financing.

## Know Your Customer (KYC)

### Customer Identification
- Verify the identity of all customers and beneficial owners before establishing a business relationship
- For corporations: obtain registration certificate, articles of incorporation, identification of directors and beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
- For individuals: government-issued photo ID plus proof of address

### Enhanced Due Diligence
Enhanced due diligence is required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions (per FATF grey/black list)
- Complex or unusually large transactions
- Transactions with no apparent economic purpose

## Transaction Monitoring

### Red Flags
- Transactions structured to avoid reporting thresholds
- Rapid movement of funds through multiple accounts
- Transactions inconsistent with customer's known business profile
- Cash-intensive businesses with unexplained deposit patterns
- Reluctance to provide identification or beneficial ownership information

### Reporting
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) must be filed with JAFIC within the prescribed timeframe
- Do not inform the customer that an STR has been filed ("tipping off" is prohibited)
- All transaction monitoring alerts must be investigated and documented

## Record Retention

- Customer identification records: 7 years after end of relationship
- Transaction records: 7 years from date of transaction
- STR records: 7 years from date of filing

## Training

All employees in customer-facing and financial roles must complete AML training within 30 days of hiring and annually thereafter.
