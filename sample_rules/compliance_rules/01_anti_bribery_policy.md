# Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the Company's zero-tolerance position on bribery and corruption, in compliance with the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), UK Bribery Act 2010, and Japan's Unfair Competition Prevention Act.

## Scope

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, agents, and any person acting on behalf of the Company worldwide.

## Prohibitions

1. **No bribes.** No employee shall offer, promise, give, or authorize the giving of anything of value to any person — government official or private — to influence their actions or obtain an improper business advantage.

2. **No facilitation payments.** The Company does not permit facilitation payments regardless of local custom. There is no de minimis exception.

3. **No kickbacks.** Employees must not accept or offer kickbacks, secret commissions, or undisclosed payments in connection with any business transaction.

## Gifts and Entertainment

- Business gifts must not exceed 10,000 JPY per occasion
- Gifts above 5,000 JPY must be reported to the compliance team
- Cash or cash-equivalent gifts (gift cards) are never permitted
- Entertainment of government officials requires prior compliance approval
- All gifts and entertainment must be accurately recorded in the books and records

## Third-Party Due Diligence

- All agents, consultants, and intermediaries must undergo anti-corruption screening before engagement
- Contracts must include anti-corruption representations, warranties, and audit rights
- Commission rates must be commercially reasonable and benchmarked
- Red flags (e.g., requests for unusual payment terms, cash payments, offshore accounts) must be reported

## Reporting

- Report suspected violations to the Chief Compliance Officer or the anonymous hotline
- Retaliation against good-faith reporters is strictly prohibited
- Failure to report known violations is itself a policy violation

## Consequences

Violations may result in immediate termination, clawback of compensation, and referral to law enforcement.
