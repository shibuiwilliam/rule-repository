# Communication Rules — Messaging Policy

Rules governing internal and external communication via chat, email, and
collaboration platforms.

## Confidential Information

Employees MUST NOT share confidential information (trade secrets, unreleased
product details, customer data, financial projections) in public or external
communication channels. Violations may result in disciplinary action.

## Credentials and Secrets

API keys, passwords, access tokens, and other credentials MUST NOT be
shared via chat, email, or any messaging platform. Use the organization's
approved secrets management tool (e.g., Vault, 1Password) instead.

## External Communication

Messages to external parties (clients, vendors, press) MUST be reviewed
and approved by the relevant department head before sending. Exceptions:
routine operational communication within established business relationships.

## Harassment and Conduct

All communication MUST comply with the organization's code of conduct.
Messages containing harassment, discrimination, threats, or inappropriate
content are prohibited regardless of the channel or audience.

## Regulated Channels

Discussions involving regulated topics (financial advice, legal opinions,
medical information) MUST take place in designated recorded channels
that meet compliance retention requirements. If a regulated discussion
begins in a non-recorded channel, participants SHOULD move it to the
appropriate channel immediately.
