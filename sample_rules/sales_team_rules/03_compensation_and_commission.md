# Compensation, Commission, and Incentive Policy

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** SALES-003
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01
**Owner:** VP of Sales, HR Department
**Approved by:** CRO, CFO
**Review Cycle:** Annual (with mid-year adjustment window)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the commission structure, quota assignment, payment rules, and incentive programs for the YourExampleCompany sales organization. Transparent compensation rules ensure fairness, motivate high performance, and align individual incentives with company revenue goals.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all commission-eligible sales roles:
- Account Executives (AEs)
- Senior Account Executives
- Sales Development Representatives (SDRs)
- Channel/Partner Sales Managers
- Sales Managers (overlay commission on team performance)

Non-commission-eligible roles (Sales Engineers, Sales Operations) are governed by their respective compensation plans.

---

## 3. Compensation Structure

### 3.1 On-Target Earnings (OTE)

- OTE MUST be communicated to each sales team member in writing at the start of each fiscal year or upon role change.
- The OTE split for AEs MUST be 50% base salary and 50% variable (commission). Deviations from this split MUST be approved by the CRO.
- SDR OTE MUST be split 70% base salary and 30% variable (bonus).
- MUST NOT reduce an employee's OTE during a fiscal year without mutual written agreement, except in cases of role change or documented performance issues.

### 3.2 Quota Assignment

- Individual quotas MUST be communicated in writing within 30 days of the fiscal year start or the employee's start date, whichever is later.
- Quotas MUST be achievable. At least 60% of the sales team SHOULD have achieved 80% or more of quota in the previous year for the quota methodology to be considered valid.
- MUST provide a ramp quota for new hires: 25% of full quota in Month 1-2, 50% in Month 3-4, 75% in Month 5-6, and 100% from Month 7 onward.
- Quota adjustments mid-year MUST be approved by the CRO and communicated at least 30 days before the start of the affected quarter. MUST NOT retroactively increase quotas for periods already completed.
- MUST NOT assign quota on products, services, or territories where the AE has no reasonable ability to generate revenue (e.g., products not yet launched in the AE's territory).

## 4. Commission Calculation

### 4.1 Commissionable Revenue

- Commissions MUST be calculated on net new Annual Contract Value (ACV) for new logos and expansion revenue.
- Renewal revenue MUST be commissioned at a lower rate than new business (see rate table below) to incentivize hunting.
- MUST NOT pay commission on revenue that has been invoiced but not collected within 120 days of the invoice date. Commission for such revenue will be held and paid upon collection.
- MUST NOT pay commission on internal transfers, proof-of-concept engagements, or zero-value contracts.

### 4.2 Commission Rates

| Revenue Type | Base Rate (at 100% quota) | Accelerator (>100% quota) |
|-------------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| New Logo ACV | 10% | 15% (for revenue above 100% attainment) |
| Expansion ACV (existing customer, new scope) | 8% | 12% |
| Renewal ACV | 4% | 4% (no accelerator on renewals) |
| Multi-year (2+ year) Premium | +2% on first-year ACV | +3% |

### 4.3 Accelerators and Decelerators

- Accelerators MUST activate only after the AE reaches 100% of quarterly quota. Revenue above 100% attainment earns the accelerator rate.
- Overachievement commission is uncapped. MUST NOT impose earning caps on individual AEs.
- For attainment below 50% of quarterly quota, a decelerator of 0.5x the base rate applies. This means an AE at 40% attainment earns commission at 5% (not 10%) on new logo ACV.
- MUST NOT apply decelerators retroactively if an AE reaches 50% attainment later in the quarter.

### 4.4 Commission Splits

- MUST document all commission splits in the CRM Deal Registration before the deal reaches S4 (Proposal).
- Split requests submitted after S4 MUST be approved by the VP of Sales and MUST NOT modify the original AE's share without their written consent.
- The standard SDR-AE split is 20% to SDR and 80% to AE on the first-year ACV of SDR-sourced opportunities.
- For partner-sourced deals, the channel commission (paid to the partner) MUST NOT reduce the AE's commission. Partner fees are funded from a separate partner budget.

## 5. Payment Schedule

### 5.1 Payment Timing

- Commission MUST be calculated monthly and paid in the payroll cycle following the month in which the booking is recognized.
- "Booking recognized" means: (a) a signed contract exists, (b) the deal is marked Closed-Won in the CRM, and (c) Finance has confirmed the booking amount.
- If any of the three recognition criteria are not met by the last business day of the month, the commission payment MUST be deferred to the following month.
- MUST pay commission within 45 days of booking recognition. Payments delayed beyond 45 days MUST include a written explanation to the AE.

### 5.2 Draws and Advances

- New hires in their ramp period MAY receive a non-recoverable draw against commission for the first 3 months, equal to 50% of their monthly variable target.
- Draws beyond 3 months MUST be approved by the VP of Sales and MUST be recoverable (deducted from future commission earnings).
- MUST NOT deduct recoverable draws from base salary. Draws MUST only be recovered from commission payments.

## 6. Clawback and Adjustments

### 6.1 Clawback Triggers

- MUST claw back 100% of commission paid on any deal that is cancelled, materially downsized (>25% ACV reduction), or terminated within 6 months of contract signature due to customer dissatisfaction or misrepresentation during the sales process.
- MUST claw back commission on any deal where the AE is found to have violated the pricing and discounting rules (SALES-001 Section 7) or misrepresented product capabilities.
- MUST NOT claw back commission for cancellations caused by delivery failures, product defects, or company-initiated contract changes unrelated to the AE's conduct.

### 6.2 Clawback Process

- Finance MUST notify the AE in writing within 10 business days of identifying a clawback event, including the amount, reason, and supporting evidence.
- The AE MAY dispute the clawback in writing within 10 business days of notification. Disputes MUST be reviewed by the VP of Sales and HR within 20 business days.
- Clawback deductions MUST NOT exceed 50% of any single commission payment to avoid undue financial hardship. Remaining balances MUST be spread across subsequent payments.

## 7. SPIFs and Incentive Programs

### 7.1 Sales Performance Incentive Funds (SPIFs)

- SPIFs MUST be announced in writing with clear eligibility criteria, measurement period, and payout terms before the measurement period begins.
- MUST NOT announce SPIFs retroactively (e.g., declaring a SPIF after deals have already closed).
- SPIF payouts MUST be made within 30 days of the measurement period end.
- MUST NOT offer SPIFs that incentivize behavior contrary to other sales policies (e.g., a SPIF for closing deals quickly that encourages skipping qualification steps).

### 7.2 President's Club and Annual Awards

- President's Club qualification MUST be based on transparent, measurable criteria (e.g., annual quota attainment, customer satisfaction score, CRM compliance).
- Qualification criteria MUST be published within 30 days of the fiscal year start and MUST NOT change mid-year.
- MUST include at least one qualitative criterion (e.g., teamwork, mentorship, CRM hygiene) in addition to revenue attainment.

---

## 8. Territory Changes and Role Transitions

### 8.1 Territory Reassignment

- When a territory is reassigned, MUST pay commission to the outgoing AE on all deals in S4 or later at the time of reassignment, regardless of who closes the deal.
- For deals in S1-S3, commission MUST follow the deal to the new territory owner.
- MUST provide 30 days written notice before a territory change takes effect, except in cases of AE departure.

### 8.2 Departure

- Upon voluntary or involuntary departure, MUST pay commission on all deals that are Closed-Won as of the departure date.
- Deals in S5 (Negotiation) or S6 (Closing) at the time of departure MUST be eligible for 50% commission if closed within 60 days of departure, provided the departing AE contributed materially to the deal.
- MUST NOT withhold earned commission as leverage during the offboarding process.

---

## 9. Dispute Resolution

- All commission disputes MUST be submitted in writing to Sales Operations within 30 days of the disputed payment (or non-payment).
- Sales Operations MUST acknowledge the dispute within 5 business days and provide a resolution within 20 business days.
- If the AE disagrees with the resolution, MUST escalate to the VP of Sales for final determination. The VP's decision is final and binding.
- MUST maintain records of all commission calculations and disputes for a minimum of 3 years.

---

## 10. Confidentiality

- Individual commission rates, quota numbers, and earnings MUST be treated as confidential. MUST NOT disclose personal compensation details to colleagues or external parties.
- The overall compensation structure and rate tables in this policy MAY be shared with all commission-eligible employees.
- MUST NOT use compensation information as a negotiating tool with customers (e.g., "I need this deal to hit my quota").
