# Sales Process and Pipeline Management

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** SALES-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01
**Owner:** VP of Sales
**Approved by:** CRO, CEO
**Review Cycle:** Semi-annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the mandatory sales process, pipeline management rules, and deal hygiene standards for all members of the YourExampleCompany sales organization. Consistent pipeline discipline ensures accurate forecasting, fair territory management, and repeatable revenue growth.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All Account Executives (AEs)
- Sales Development Representatives (SDRs)
- Sales Engineers (SEs)
- Sales Managers and Directors
- Channel and Partner Sales representatives
- Any employee involved in revenue-generating activities

---

## 3. Sales Stages and Definitions

### 3.1 Stage Definitions

All opportunities MUST follow the standard seven-stage pipeline:

| Stage | Name | Entry Criteria | Exit Criteria |
|-------|------|----------------|---------------|
| S1 | Prospecting | Identified target account with qualified contact | Discovery call scheduled |
| S2 | Discovery | Discovery call completed | Pain points documented, budget range confirmed |
| S3 | Solution Design | Technical requirements gathered | Solution proposal drafted, SE assigned |
| S4 | Proposal | Proposal delivered to economic buyer | Verbal or written feedback received |
| S5 | Negotiation | Commercial terms under discussion | Redline or final terms agreed |
| S6 | Closing | Contract sent for signature | Signed contract received |
| S7 | Won | Signed contract and PO received | Handoff to delivery completed |

### 3.2 Stage Progression Rules

- MUST NOT skip stages. Every opportunity MUST pass through each stage in order.
- MUST NOT move an opportunity to a later stage until all entry criteria for that stage are documented in the CRM.
- MUST update the CRM stage within 1 business day of the actual stage change event.
- MUST NOT leave an opportunity in the same stage for more than 30 calendar days without a documented reason and manager approval.

### 3.3 Stale Deal Management

- MUST mark any opportunity with no customer activity for 45 calendar days as "Stalled."
- MUST move any opportunity that has been "Stalled" for an additional 30 days to "Closed-Lost" unless the AE provides a written reactivation plan approved by the Sales Manager.
- MUST NOT keep deals in the pipeline solely to inflate pipeline coverage numbers.

## 4. CRM Hygiene and Data Quality

### 4.1 Required Fields

Every opportunity record MUST have the following fields populated before leaving S2 (Discovery):
- **Account Name** and **Primary Contact** with valid email and phone number
- **Estimated Annual Contract Value (ACV)** in the company's reporting currency (JPY)
- **Expected Close Date** — a realistic date, not the last day of the quarter
- **Budget Confirmed** — Yes/No with supporting notes
- **Decision Maker Identified** — name and title of the economic buyer
- **Competition** — known competitors or "No Competition" with justification
- **Next Step** — a specific, time-bound action (not generic text like "follow up")

### 4.2 Data Accuracy

- MUST NOT set the Expected Close Date to the last day of the current quarter unless there is a documented, verifiable reason (e.g., customer's fiscal year-end procurement deadline).
- MUST update the Expected Close Date whenever the timeline changes, not only at forecast submission time.
- MUST NOT inflate the ACV estimate. The ACV MUST reflect the most likely contract value based on current discussions, not the maximum possible upsell.
- MUST log every meaningful customer interaction (calls, emails, meetings) in the CRM within 2 business days of occurrence.

### 4.3 Contact and Stakeholder Mapping

- MUST map at least three contacts per opportunity by the time the deal reaches S3 (Solution Design): the economic buyer, a technical evaluator, and an internal champion.
- MUST identify the procurement process and any third-party involvement (e.g., legal review, purchasing department) by S4 (Proposal).
- SHOULD document the organizational decision-making structure (MEDDIC or equivalent framework) for deals above 10 million JPY ACV.

## 5. Forecasting

### 5.1 Forecast Categories

All AEs MUST assign each opportunity to exactly one forecast category:

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **Commit** | AE has high confidence (>90%) that the deal will close this quarter at the stated ACV. Verbal or written commitment from the customer exists. |
| **Best Case** | AE believes (60-89%) the deal can close this quarter, but one or more variables remain unresolved. |
| **Pipeline** | The deal is progressing but unlikely (<60%) to close this quarter. |
| **Omit** | The deal will not close this quarter. |

### 5.2 Forecast Submission

- MUST submit an updated forecast every Monday by 10:00 AM JST.
- MUST NOT change the Commit category for any deal after Wednesday of the last week of the quarter without VP-level approval.
- MUST include a written explanation in the CRM notes whenever a deal moves from Commit to a lower category or is pushed to the next quarter.

### 5.3 Forecast Accuracy Accountability

- Quarterly forecast accuracy (Commit vs. actual closed) below 70% for two consecutive quarters MUST trigger a mandatory pipeline review with the VP of Sales.
- MUST NOT sandbag deals. Deliberately under-forecasting to create artificial "upside" is a violation of this policy and will be treated as a performance concern.

## 6. Deal Qualification

### 6.1 Qualification Framework

All opportunities MUST be qualified using the MEDDIC framework:

- **Metrics**: What measurable outcomes does the customer expect?
- **Economic Buyer**: Who controls the budget and can authorize the purchase?
- **Decision Criteria**: What criteria will the customer use to evaluate vendors?
- **Decision Process**: What steps, approvals, and timeline does the customer follow?
- **Identify Pain**: What specific business pain does the customer need to resolve?
- **Champion**: Is there an internal advocate who will sell on our behalf?

### 6.2 Qualification Thresholds

- MUST NOT advance an opportunity beyond S3 (Solution Design) unless at least four of the six MEDDIC elements are documented in the CRM.
- MUST complete all six MEDDIC elements before moving an opportunity to S5 (Negotiation).
- SHOULD disqualify and close opportunities where no economic buyer has been identified after three discovery attempts.

### 6.3 Minimum Deal Size

- MUST NOT pursue new-logo opportunities with an ACV below 1 million JPY without Sales Manager approval. Below this threshold, the customer SHOULD be directed to self-service or partner channels.
- MAY pursue renewal or expansion deals below 1 million JPY ACV without additional approval.

## 7. Pricing and Discounting

### 7.1 Standard Pricing

- MUST use the current published price list for all proposals. Custom pricing MUST NOT be offered without written approval.
- MUST NOT share internal cost structures, margin targets, or partner pricing with customers or prospects.

### 7.2 Discount Authority

| Discount Level | Approval Required |
|----------------|-------------------|
| 0-10% | AE (self-approved, documented in CRM) |
| 11-20% | Sales Manager |
| 21-30% | VP of Sales |
| >30% | CRO with CFO countersign |

- MUST document the business justification for any discount above 10% in the CRM before requesting approval.
- MUST NOT offer discounts contingent on the customer signing by a specific date ("end-of-quarter pressure discounts") unless pre-approved by the VP of Sales as part of a documented promotion.
- MUST NOT retroactively apply discounts to already-signed contracts.

### 7.3 Non-Standard Terms

- MUST escalate any request for payment terms beyond Net-30 to the Finance team for credit review before committing to the customer.
- MUST NOT agree to unlimited liability, uncapped SLA penalties, or source code escrow clauses without Legal review.
- MUST NOT offer free pilot engagements exceeding 30 days or 5 million JPY in estimated delivery cost without VP of Sales approval.

## 8. Territory and Account Assignment

### 8.1 Territory Boundaries

- MUST respect assigned territory boundaries (geographic or named-account). MUST NOT prospect or engage accounts assigned to another AE without prior written agreement.
- MUST escalate territory disputes to the Sales Manager within 3 business days of discovery. The Sales Manager MUST resolve disputes within 5 business days.
- MUST NOT split commissions informally. All split arrangements MUST be documented in a Deal Registration form approved by the Sales Manager before the deal reaches S4.

### 8.2 Account Ownership Expiration

- MUST relinquish ownership of any named account where no opportunity has been created within 90 days of assignment, unless a documented account plan is on file and approved by the Sales Manager.
- MAY request a 90-day extension by submitting an updated account plan with specific milestones.

---

## 9. Handoffs and Collaboration

### 9.1 SDR-to-AE Handoff

- SDRs MUST provide a completed Qualification Handoff Form before scheduling a discovery call for the AE. The form MUST include: prospect name, title, company, confirmed pain point, and proposed meeting agenda.
- AEs MUST accept or reject the qualified meeting within 4 business hours of handoff during business days.
- AEs MUST NOT reject qualified meetings without documenting the rejection reason in the CRM.

### 9.2 Sales-to-Delivery Handoff

- MUST complete a signed Statement of Work (SOW) before initiating the delivery handoff.
- MUST conduct a formal handoff meeting with the Delivery Manager within 5 business days of contract signature. The meeting MUST cover: customer expectations, scope, timeline, key contacts, and any commitments made during the sales process that affect delivery.
- MUST NOT make technical commitments, custom development promises, or timeline guarantees to customers without written confirmation from the Delivery team during the sales cycle.

### 9.3 Sales Engineer Engagement

- MUST submit an SE Request at least 3 business days before a scheduled technical meeting.
- MUST NOT bring an SE to a meeting without a documented agenda and clear technical objectives.
- SHOULD debrief with the SE within 1 business day after a joint customer meeting to capture technical requirements and action items.

---

## 10. Compliance and Ethics

### 10.1 Anti-Bribery

- MUST NOT offer, promise, or provide gifts, entertainment, or anything of value to any customer or prospect employee to influence a purchasing decision.
- MUST NOT accept gifts from vendors or partners valued above 5,000 JPY without reporting to the Compliance Officer.
- MUST comply with all applicable anti-bribery laws, including the Unfair Competition Prevention Act (Japan) and the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act where applicable.

### 10.2 Competitive Intelligence

- MUST NOT misrepresent competitor products, pricing, or capabilities to customers.
- MUST NOT use confidential information obtained from former employees of competitors.
- SHOULD use only publicly available information and authorized competitive intelligence tools.

### 10.3 Customer Data Handling

- MUST NOT store customer proprietary information (RFP documents, financial data, infrastructure diagrams) on personal devices or unauthorized cloud storage.
- MUST use only company-approved tools (CRM, company Google Workspace, approved file shares) for storing and sharing customer information.
- MUST delete or return all customer-provided evaluation materials within 30 days of a Closed-Lost outcome unless the customer grants written permission to retain them.

---

## 11. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Commission clawback for deals closed in violation of pricing or discounting rules
- Pipeline audit and remediation plan for CRM hygiene violations
- Written warning for first offense, performance improvement plan for repeated violations
- Termination for fraud, bribery, or deliberate data falsification

All sales team members MUST acknowledge this policy in writing upon joining the team and annually thereafter.
