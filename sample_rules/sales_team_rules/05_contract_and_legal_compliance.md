# Contract and Legal Compliance Standards

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** SALES-005
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01
**Owner:** VP of Sales, General Counsel
**Approved by:** CRO, General Counsel, CFO
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the rules governing contract execution, legal review requirements, regulatory compliance, and intellectual property protections in the sales process. Compliance with these rules protects YourExampleCompany from legal liability and ensures enforceable, profitable agreements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all sales team members involved in contract negotiation, execution, or amendment. It also applies to Sales Managers who approve non-standard terms and to anyone who communicates contractual commitments to customers.

---

## 3. Contract Execution Authority

### 3.1 Signature Authority

Only the following individuals are authorized to sign contracts on behalf of YourExampleCompany:

| Contract Value (ACV) | Authorized Signers |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| Up to 10M JPY | Sales Manager |
| 10M - 50M JPY | VP of Sales |
| 50M - 100M JPY | CRO |
| Above 100M JPY | CEO or COO |

- MUST NOT sign contracts or binding agreements (including LOIs, MOUs, and side letters) without proper signature authority.
- MUST NOT use electronic signatures for contracts above 50M JPY ACV unless the customer's procurement process explicitly accepts them and Legal has approved.
- MUST obtain signature authority delegation in writing when the authorized signer is unavailable for more than 5 business days.

### 3.2 Pre-Signature Review

- MUST obtain Legal review for any contract that includes non-standard terms (see Section 4).
- MUST obtain Finance review for any deal with payment terms beyond Net-30, milestone-based payments, or revenue recognition implications.
- MUST NOT execute a contract that has been modified from the approved template without Legal sign-off on the redlined version.
- MUST retain a fully executed copy (all pages, all signatures) in the contract management system within 2 business days of execution.

## 4. Standard vs. Non-Standard Terms

### 4.1 Standard Terms

The following terms are pre-approved and MUST be used as the default for all new contracts:
- Payment Terms: Net-30
- Liability Cap: 12 months of fees paid under the contract
- Warranty Period: 90 days from acceptance
- Termination: 30 days written notice for convenience (annual contracts); 60 days for multi-year
- Intellectual Property: YourExampleCompany retains all IP in pre-existing and reusable deliverables; customer receives a perpetual license to custom deliverables
- Governing Law: Laws of Japan, Tokyo District Court exclusive jurisdiction
- Indemnification: Mutual, limited to third-party IP infringement claims

### 4.2 Non-Standard Terms Requiring Legal Review

The following modifications MUST be reviewed by Legal before agreeing with the customer:
- Liability cap below 6 months of fees or unlimited liability
- Consequential damages not excluded
- Indemnification obligations beyond IP infringement (e.g., data breach, regulatory fines)
- SLA penalties or service credits exceeding 15% of monthly fees
- Audit rights that include access to YourExampleCompany's cost structures or subcontractor agreements
- Source code escrow obligations
- Non-compete or exclusivity clauses
- Assignment restrictions (either party)
- Data processing agreements or cross-border data transfer provisions
- Any terms governed by non-Japanese law or subject to non-Japanese jurisdiction

### 4.3 Prohibited Terms

MUST NOT agree to any of the following under any circumstances without CEO approval:
- Unlimited or uncapped liability
- Guarantees of specific business outcomes or ROI
- "Most Favored Customer" pricing clauses
- Automatic renewal with price escalation caps below CPI
- Blanket rights for the customer to use YourExampleCompany's IP in perpetuity without limitation
- Non-solicitation clauses that extend beyond the contract term plus 12 months

## 5. Order Forms and SOWs

### 5.1 Order Form Requirements

- Every revenue-generating engagement MUST be documented in a signed Order Form or SOW that references the Master Services Agreement (MSA).
- MUST NOT begin billable work before an Order Form or SOW is fully executed (signed by both parties).
- The Order Form MUST specify: services description, deliverables, timeline, fees, payment schedule, and acceptance criteria.
- MUST NOT use vague scope descriptions (e.g., "consulting services as needed"). Scope MUST be specific enough to determine whether work is in-scope or out-of-scope.

### 5.2 Change Orders

- Any change to scope, timeline, or fees after SOW execution MUST be documented in a signed Change Order.
- MUST NOT perform out-of-scope work based on verbal customer requests. Verbal requests MUST be documented in a Change Order before work begins.
- MUST NOT waive Change Order fees without Sales Manager approval. Change Orders for work valued above 5M JPY require VP of Sales approval.

### 5.3 Multi-Year Contracts

- Multi-year contracts (2+ years) MUST include an annual price adjustment clause (minimum CPI-linked, or 3% annual increase, whichever is greater).
- MUST NOT lock pricing for more than 3 years without CFO approval.
- Multi-year contracts MUST include a termination-for-convenience clause allowing either party to exit with 90 days notice after the first year.
- MUST document the total contract value (TCV) and annualized ACV separately in the CRM.

## 6. NDA and Confidentiality

### 6.1 NDA Requirements

- MUST execute a mutual NDA before sharing any proprietary information with a prospect, including pricing, methodology, case studies with identifiable details, or technology architecture.
- MUST use the YourExampleCompany standard NDA template. Customer-provided NDAs MUST be reviewed by Legal.
- MUST NOT sign NDAs with non-standard terms (e.g., NDA term exceeding 5 years, obligations surviving beyond 3 years after termination) without Legal review.

### 6.2 Confidentiality Obligations

- MUST NOT share one customer's confidential information with another customer, even in anonymized form, without express written permission.
- MUST NOT use customer-provided data, architectures, or processes as templates or inputs for other customer engagements.
- MUST NOT discuss customer names, deal values, or engagement details with competitors, analysts, or press without CMO approval.
- MUST mark all confidential customer materials as "Confidential" when stored in internal systems.

---

## 7. Regulatory Compliance

### 7.1 Data Protection

- MUST comply with the Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI) in all sales activities involving Japanese residents' personal data.
- MUST execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with any customer whose engagement involves handling personal data on their behalf, before data processing begins.
- MUST consult with the Privacy Officer before proposing any engagement that involves cross-border transfer of personal data (e.g., using overseas cloud infrastructure for a Japanese customer's data).
- MUST NOT collect or store personal data from prospects beyond what is necessary for the sales process (name, title, company, business contact information).

### 7.2 Industry-Specific Regulations

- MUST identify industry-specific regulatory requirements (FSA regulations for financial services, MHLW guidelines for healthcare, etc.) during the Discovery phase for customers in regulated industries.
- MUST involve the Compliance team in proposals for customers in financial services, healthcare, government, or critical infrastructure sectors.
- MUST NOT make compliance claims (e.g., "Our solution is FISC-compliant") without written validation from the Product or Compliance team.

### 7.3 Export Controls

- MUST verify that proposed services and deliverables do not violate Japanese export control regulations (Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Act) when selling to customers with international operations.
- MUST consult Legal before engaging with customers headquartered in countries subject to international sanctions.
- MUST NOT provide technical consulting services that could be classified as "technology transfer" under export control regulations without Legal clearance.

## 8. Intellectual Property

### 8.1 Pre-Existing IP

- MUST clearly identify all pre-existing YourExampleCompany IP (frameworks, tools, methodologies, code libraries) in the SOW before project start.
- MUST NOT transfer ownership of pre-existing IP to customers. Customers receive a license to use deliverables incorporating pre-existing IP, not ownership.
- MUST NOT agree to "work-for-hire" clauses that assign all IP created during an engagement to the customer without Legal review and CRO approval.

### 8.2 Customer IP

- MUST respect all customer IP rights. MUST NOT incorporate customer-owned code, designs, or data into YourExampleCompany's reusable assets without explicit written permission.
- MUST return or destroy all customer IP upon project completion or contract termination, per the contract terms.

### 8.3 Open Source

- MUST disclose the use of open-source components in deliverables to the customer before contract execution.
- MUST NOT include open-source components with copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL) in customer deliverables without Legal review and customer acknowledgment.
- MUST maintain an open-source component inventory for all deliverables and provide it to the customer upon request.

---

## 9. Contract Amendments and Renewals

### 9.1 Amendments

- All contract amendments MUST be in writing, signed by authorized representatives of both parties.
- MUST NOT agree to verbal amendments, even for minor changes. If a customer requests a verbal change, MUST respond with "I'll document that in a formal amendment for both of us."
- MUST track all amendments in the contract management system with clear linkage to the original agreement.

### 9.2 Renewal Process

- MUST initiate the renewal conversation at least 90 days before contract expiration for annual contracts and 180 days for multi-year contracts.
- MUST NOT allow contracts to auto-renew at below-market rates without a proactive review. Every renewal MUST be an opportunity to right-size pricing.
- MUST document renewal terms (including any price changes) in a signed Renewal Order Form before the current term expires.
- MUST NOT allow service to continue after contract expiration without a signed renewal or extension letter. Continued service without a contract creates legal and financial risk.

---

## 10. Record Retention

- MUST retain all executed contracts, amendments, and related correspondence for a minimum of 7 years after contract termination, per Japanese Commercial Code requirements.
- MUST retain all NDA documents for the duration of the confidentiality obligation plus 3 years.
- MUST store all contract records in the approved contract management system. Local copies on personal drives MUST be treated as unofficial and deleted upon upload to the central system.
- MUST NOT delete or modify contract records without Legal approval.

---

## 11. Enforcement

- Contract execution without proper authority is a serious policy violation that may result in immediate disciplinary action, including termination.
- Sales team members who commit to non-standard terms without Legal review MUST be reported to the VP of Sales and General Counsel.
- Deals executed in violation of this policy MAY be subject to commission clawback (see SALES-003 Section 6).
- All sales team members MUST complete annual contract compliance training administered by Legal. Failure to complete training by the deadline MUST result in suspension of deal closing authority until training is completed.
