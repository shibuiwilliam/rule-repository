# Customer Engagement and Communication Standards

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** SALES-002
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01
**Owner:** VP of Sales
**Approved by:** CRO, CMO
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes standards for all customer-facing communication, meeting conduct, proposal quality, and engagement etiquette. Consistent, professional engagement builds trust and reinforces YourExampleCompany's brand as a premium IT consulting partner.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all sales team members as defined in SALES-001 Section 2, and extends to any employee who communicates with customers or prospects on behalf of the sales organization (including Marketing, Pre-Sales, and Executive Sponsors).

---

## 3. Communication Standards

### 3.1 Response Time

- MUST respond to all inbound customer inquiries (email, phone, web form) within 4 business hours during standard business hours (9:00-18:00 JST, Monday-Friday).
- MUST acknowledge receipt of RFPs, RFIs, and formal requests within 1 business day, even if the full response requires more time.
- MUST NOT leave a customer email unanswered for more than 2 business days. If a substantive response is not yet ready, MUST send an acknowledgment with an expected timeline.
- SHOULD respond to partner-referred leads within 2 business hours to maintain partner confidence.

### 3.2 Email Standards

- MUST use the company email address for all business correspondence. MUST NOT use personal email accounts for customer communication.
- MUST include a professional email signature with full name, title, direct phone number, and company website.
- MUST NOT use informal greetings (e.g., "Hey," "Yo") in initial outreach to new contacts. Professional salutations (e.g., "Dear [Name]-san," "Hello [Name]-san") are required.
- MUST proofread all customer-facing emails for spelling, grammar, and factual accuracy before sending.
- MUST NOT send bulk or mass emails to customer contacts without Marketing team approval and CAN-SPAM / Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI) compliance review.
- MUST BCC the CRM email capture address on all customer emails to ensure interaction logging.

### 3.3 Phone and Video Calls

- MUST confirm meeting time, attendees, and agenda via email at least 24 hours before a scheduled customer call.
- MUST join video calls from a professional environment with appropriate lighting, background, and minimal background noise.
- MUST NOT record customer calls without explicit verbal or written consent from all participants at the start of the recording.
- MUST send a written follow-up summary within 1 business day after every customer call, including agreed action items, owners, and deadlines.
- SHOULD keep calls focused on the stated agenda. If new topics arise, MUST schedule a separate follow-up rather than extending the call beyond the scheduled time without customer agreement.

### 3.4 Written Communication Tone

- MUST maintain a professional, consultative tone in all communications. The goal is to be a trusted advisor, not a transactional vendor.
- MUST NOT use high-pressure language (e.g., "This offer expires today," "You'll regret missing this," "Your competitor just signed").
- MUST NOT disparage competitors by name. Focus on YourExampleCompany's strengths and differentiation.
- MUST use the customer's preferred language (Japanese or English) for all formal communications. MAY use English internally but MUST translate customer-facing materials.

## 4. Meeting Conduct

### 4.1 Preparation

- MUST research the customer's company, industry, recent news, and key stakeholders before every external meeting.
- MUST prepare a meeting agenda with specific objectives and share it with internal team members at least 24 hours before the meeting.
- MUST review the CRM opportunity record and all previous interaction notes before every customer meeting to avoid asking questions the customer has already answered.
- MUST NOT attend a customer meeting without at least one prepared question or discussion point specific to the customer's business situation.

### 4.2 During the Meeting

- MUST arrive on time. For in-person meetings, arrive 10 minutes early. For video calls, join 2 minutes early to verify audio and video.
- MUST introduce all YourExampleCompany attendees by name and role at the start of the meeting.
- MUST NOT use mobile phones for non-meeting purposes during customer meetings.
- MUST take notes during the meeting. If an SE or additional team member is present, MUST designate a note-taker in advance.
- MUST NOT make commitments about pricing, timeline, or capabilities that have not been validated internally. Use language such as "I'll confirm and follow up" rather than making unverified promises.
- SHOULD ask permission before sharing the customer's screen or presenting in their environment.

### 4.3 Post-Meeting

- MUST distribute a meeting summary to all attendees (internal and external) within 1 business day.
- MUST update the CRM with meeting notes, updated stage (if applicable), and next steps within 1 business day.
- MUST complete all action items assigned during the meeting by their agreed deadlines, or proactively notify the customer of delays before the deadline passes.
- MUST NOT wait for the customer to follow up on open action items. Proactively provide updates on a weekly cadence for active deals.

## 5. Proposal and Presentation Standards

### 5.1 Proposal Format

- MUST use the current approved proposal template (available in the Sales Enablement portal). MUST NOT create custom formats without Marketing approval.
- MUST include the following sections in every proposal:
  1. Executive Summary tailored to the customer's stated business objectives
  2. Understanding of the customer's current challenges and requirements
  3. Proposed solution with scope, deliverables, and timeline
  4. Team composition and relevant experience
  5. Pricing with clear line items
  6. Terms and conditions (referencing the standard MSA)
  7. References or case studies (with permission from referenced customers)

### 5.2 Content Quality

- MUST verify all technical claims and statistics in the proposal with the Pre-Sales or Delivery team before submission.
- MUST NOT include logos or names of reference customers without their written consent.
- MUST NOT copy-paste content from proposals for other customers without thorough review and customization. Generic proposals that do not address the specific customer's needs are prohibited.
- MUST have proposals for deals above 20 million JPY ACV reviewed by the Sales Manager before submission.
- SHOULD have proposals for deals above 50 million JPY ACV reviewed by the VP of Sales.

### 5.3 Presentation Delivery

- MUST use the current corporate slide template for all customer presentations.
- MUST rehearse presentations for deals above 20 million JPY ACV at least once with an internal audience.
- MUST NOT read slides verbatim during presentations. Slides SHOULD support the narrative, not replace it.
- MUST prepare answers for at least five anticipated customer objections before any proposal presentation.

## 6. Demo and Proof-of-Concept Standards

### 6.1 Demonstrations

- MUST prepare a tailored demo environment that reflects the customer's use case. Generic "canned" demos are acceptable only for initial introductory meetings.
- MUST verify the demo environment works end-to-end at least 2 hours before the scheduled demo.
- MUST NOT demo features that are not generally available (GA) without clearly disclosing their roadmap status and the caveat that timelines may change.
- MUST have a backup plan (screenshots, recorded walkthrough, or architectural diagram) in case of live demo failure.

### 6.2 Proof-of-Concept (PoC)

- MUST define clear success criteria with the customer in writing before starting any PoC.
- MUST set a fixed PoC duration (maximum 30 days) with a defined end date. MUST NOT extend PoCs without Sales Manager approval.
- MUST NOT provide production-grade support during a PoC. Support commitments begin only after contract signature.
- MUST conduct a formal PoC review meeting with the customer at the conclusion, documenting whether success criteria were met.
- MUST NOT allow PoCs to convert into unpaid ongoing usage. If the customer does not sign within 15 business days of PoC completion, MUST decommission the PoC environment.

## 7. Social Media and Public Communication

### 7.1 Social Media

- MAY use personal LinkedIn profiles to share company content, industry insights, and thought leadership relevant to sales activities.
- MUST NOT disclose customer names, deal details, contract values, or confidential business information on social media.
- MUST NOT post negative comments about competitors, industry analysts, or market conditions on social media in a professional capacity.
- MUST include a disclaimer ("Views are my own") when posting personal opinions on professional topics.

### 7.2 Conference and Event Participation

- MUST obtain Sales Manager approval before attending industry events as a company representative.
- MUST submit a post-event summary within 3 business days, including leads generated, customer meetings held, and competitive intelligence gathered.
- MUST log all leads collected at events in the CRM within 2 business days of the event.

---

## 8. Customer Escalation Handling

### 8.1 Escalation Process

- MUST NOT ignore or delay response to customer complaints, even during active sales negotiations.
- MUST acknowledge any customer escalation within 2 business hours and provide an initial assessment within 1 business day.
- MUST involve the Sales Manager for escalations that threaten an active deal or the broader customer relationship.
- MUST NOT promise specific resolutions or timelines for escalations without coordinating with the relevant internal team (Delivery, Support, Product).

### 8.2 At-Risk Accounts

- MUST notify the Sales Manager immediately when a customer indicates they are considering cancellation, non-renewal, or switching to a competitor.
- MUST create a documented retention plan within 5 business days for any at-risk account with ACV above 5 million JPY.
- SHOULD schedule an executive sponsor call for at-risk accounts above 20 million JPY ACV within 10 business days.

---

## 9. Enforcement

Violations of communication standards will be addressed through:
- Coaching and feedback for first-time minor violations (e.g., missed follow-up email)
- Written warning for repeated violations or customer complaints attributed to communication failures
- Commission impact for violations that result in lost deals or damaged customer relationships
- Disciplinary action up to termination for deliberate misrepresentation, data handling violations, or anti-bribery breaches

All customer-facing communications MAY be audited by Sales Management on a quarterly basis.
