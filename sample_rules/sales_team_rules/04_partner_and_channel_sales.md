# Partner and Channel Sales Rules

**YourExampleCompany** — IT Service Consultation

**Document ID:** SALES-004
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01
**Owner:** Head of Channel Sales
**Approved by:** CRO
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the rules for engaging with resellers, referral partners, technology alliance partners, and subcontractors in sales pursuits. Structured partner engagement protects margins, ensures consistent customer experience, and prevents channel conflict.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All sales team members who interact with or sell through partners
- Channel/Partner Sales Managers
- Any employee involved in partner deal registration, co-selling, or referral management

---

## 3. Partner Tiers and Engagement Model

### 3.1 Partner Classification

All partners MUST be classified into one of the following tiers:

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Platinum** | >100M JPY annual sourced revenue, certified team, joint business plan | Priority deal registration, highest referral fees, executive sponsorship |
| **Gold** | 30-100M JPY annual sourced revenue, at least 2 certified consultants | Standard referral fees, co-marketing funds, partner portal access |
| **Silver** | <30M JPY annual sourced revenue or new partner in evaluation period | Basic referral fees, partner portal access |

- MUST review partner tier classification quarterly and communicate changes at least 30 days before the new quarter.
- MUST NOT promise tier-specific benefits to partners who have not met the qualification criteria.

### 3.2 Partner Agreement Requirements

- MUST NOT engage in revenue-generating activities with any partner that does not have a signed Partner Agreement on file with Legal.
- MUST NOT share customer data, pricing, or proprietary methodology with partners who have not signed an NDA.
- MUST verify partner agreement status in the Partner Portal before initiating any co-sell or referral engagement.

---

## 4. Deal Registration

### 4.1 Registration Process

- Partners MUST submit deal registrations through the Partner Portal. Email-based or verbal registrations MUST NOT be accepted.
- Deal registrations MUST include: customer name, opportunity description, estimated ACV, expected close date, and the partner's value-add description.
- MUST approve or reject deal registrations within 5 business days of submission. Failure to respond within 5 business days automatically approves the registration.
- MUST NOT approve overlapping deal registrations for the same customer and opportunity from different partners without resolving the conflict first.

### 4.2 Registration Protection

- Approved deal registrations provide the partner with exclusive pricing protection for 90 days.
- MUST NOT allow direct sales to pursue the same opportunity during the protection period without the registering partner's written consent.
- If the registered deal does not progress (no customer activity for 60 days), MAY revoke the registration after notifying the partner in writing with a 10-business-day cure period.
- MUST NOT revoke registrations to reallocate deals to direct sales or preferred partners without documented justification.

### 4.3 Registration Disputes

- MUST escalate registration disputes (e.g., two partners claiming the same opportunity) to the Head of Channel Sales within 3 business days.
- The Head of Channel Sales MUST resolve disputes within 10 business days using the following priority: (1) first to register, (2) depth of existing customer relationship, (3) value-add to the opportunity.
- MUST document the dispute resolution rationale in the Partner Portal for audit purposes.

---

## 5. Referral Fees and Partner Compensation

### 5.1 Referral Fee Structure

| Partner Tier | Referral Fee (% of first-year ACV) | Co-Sell Fee (% of first-year ACV) |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Platinum | 15% | 20% |
| Gold | 10% | 15% |
| Silver | 7% | 10% |

- "Referral" means the partner introduces the opportunity but YourExampleCompany leads the sales process and delivery.
- "Co-Sell" means the partner actively participates in the sales process (joint meetings, proposal contribution, technical support).
- MUST NOT pay both referral and co-sell fees on the same deal. The higher applicable rate applies.

### 5.2 Payment Terms

- Partner referral fees MUST be paid within 60 days of customer payment receipt (not contract signature).
- MUST NOT pay referral fees on revenue that has not been collected.
- MUST NOT advance referral fees before customer payment.
- MUST provide partners with a monthly statement of registered deals, status, and pending payments through the Partner Portal.

### 5.3 Prohibited Practices

- MUST NOT offer referral fees, commissions, or kickbacks to individual employees of partner organizations (as opposed to the partner company itself). All payments MUST go to the partner entity per the signed Partner Agreement.
- MUST NOT bundle or inflate deal values to increase partner payouts.
- MUST NOT offer side agreements, unofficial discounts, or under-the-table arrangements with partners.

---

## 6. Co-Selling and Joint Customer Engagement

### 6.1 Co-Sell Rules of Engagement

- MUST designate a single point of contact (AE or Channel Manager) for each co-sell opportunity. The customer MUST NOT receive conflicting messages from multiple YourExampleCompany contacts.
- MUST align on messaging, pricing, and positioning with the partner before any joint customer meeting.
- MUST NOT undermine the partner's relationship with the customer. If YourExampleCompany has a pre-existing relationship, MUST disclose this to the partner before engaging.
- MUST include the partner in all customer-facing meetings and communications related to the co-sell opportunity, unless the partner explicitly opts out.

### 6.2 Pricing in Partner Deals

- MUST use the partner-specific price list for all partner-channel deals. MUST NOT offer end-customer pricing directly to the partner's customer without the partner's knowledge.
- MUST NOT disclose partner discount levels or margins to the end customer.
- Partners MUST NOT resell below the Minimum Advertised Price (MAP) where applicable. Violations MAY result in deal registration revocation and tier review.

### 6.3 Technical Support in Partner Deals

- MUST provide pre-sales technical support (SE engagement) for partner-sourced deals on the same terms as direct deals.
- MAY charge partners for post-sales technical enablement and training at published training rates.
- MUST NOT provide free implementation support to partners as a deal incentive without VP of Sales approval.

---

## 7. Technology Alliance Partners

### 7.1 Integration and Marketplace

- MUST obtain Legal and Product team approval before listing YourExampleCompany solutions on any partner marketplace or technology platform.
- MUST NOT make claims about technology integrations or certifications that have not been validated by the Product team.
- MUST maintain current certifications and partnership status with all technology alliance partners. Lapsed certifications MUST be reported to the Head of Channel Sales within 5 business days.

### 7.2 Joint Solutions

- MUST document all joint solution offerings in a Joint Solution Brief approved by both parties before promoting to customers.
- MUST NOT develop custom integrations for partner platforms during the sales cycle without Product and Delivery team approval and a funded SOW.
- SHOULD involve the technology alliance partner in customer meetings where their platform is a key component of the proposed solution.

---

## 8. Subcontracting

### 8.1 Using Subcontractors in Sales Pursuits

- MUST NOT commit subcontractor resources in proposals or SOWs without a signed Subcontractor Agreement and confirmed resource availability.
- MUST disclose subcontractor involvement to the customer when required by the customer's procurement policy or when the subcontractor will have direct customer interaction.
- MUST NOT represent subcontractor staff as YourExampleCompany employees in proposals, org charts, or customer-facing materials.

### 8.2 Subcontractor Pricing

- MUST maintain a minimum 25% gross margin on all subcontracted work. Deals below 25% margin require CFO approval.
- MUST NOT allow subcontractors to negotiate directly with the customer on pricing, scope, or terms.

---

## 9. Channel Conflict Resolution

### 9.1 Direct vs. Partner Conflict

- When both a direct AE and a partner are pursuing the same customer, MUST apply the following resolution order:
  1. If the partner has an approved deal registration, the partner leads.
  2. If neither has a registration and the customer has an expressed preference, follow the customer's preference.
  3. If neither of the above applies, the Sales Manager decides based on which channel is most likely to win and serve the customer well.
- MUST NOT use internal knowledge of partner deal registrations to undercut the partner with a direct offer.
- MUST document all conflict resolutions in writing and notify affected parties within 5 business days.

### 9.2 Partner vs. Partner Conflict

- When two partners pursue the same customer, MUST follow the deal registration priority rules in Section 4.3.
- MUST NOT favor one partner over another based on personal relationships. Decisions MUST be based on documented criteria.

---

## 10. Partner Performance Management

### 10.1 Quarterly Business Reviews

- MUST conduct a Quarterly Business Review (QBR) with all Platinum and Gold partners.
- SHOULD conduct semi-annual reviews with Silver partners.
- QBRs MUST cover: pipeline review, deal status, revenue performance vs. targets, certification status, and joint planning for the next quarter.

### 10.2 Underperformance

- MUST notify partners in writing if they are at risk of tier demotion at least 60 days before the quarterly review.
- Partners that fail to meet minimum revenue thresholds for two consecutive quarters MUST be reviewed for tier demotion or agreement termination.
- MUST NOT terminate a partner agreement without 90 days written notice and CRO approval, except in cases of material breach (e.g., fraud, ethical violations, confidentiality breach).

---

## 11. Enforcement

- Violations of partner engagement rules by sales team members will be addressed through the standard disciplinary process (SALES-001 Section 11).
- Commission earned on deals that violated partner deal registration protections MAY be clawed back and redirected as partner compensation.
- Repeated channel conflict caused by the same AE MUST trigger a formal review by the VP of Sales and Head of Channel Sales.
