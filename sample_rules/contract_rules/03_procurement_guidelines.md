# Procurement Guidelines

Rules for vendor selection, contracting, and ongoing management.

## Vendor Selection

1. All purchases exceeding 500,000 JPY must obtain at least 3 competitive bids
2. Single-source procurement requires documented justification and VP approval
3. New vendors must complete the vendor onboarding form including: company registration, financial statements, insurance certificates, references, compliance questionnaire
4. Vendors handling personal data must complete a data protection assessment

## Purchase Orders

- All purchases must have an approved purchase order before goods/services are delivered
- PO must reference the applicable MSA or contract number
- Verbal agreements are not binding; all commitments must be documented
- PO amounts must not exceed the approved budget without re-authorization

## Contract Execution

- Standard templates must be used as the starting point for all agreements
- Deviations from standard terms require legal review
- All contracts must be signed by authorized signatories per the authority matrix
- Executed contracts must be registered in the contract management system within 5 business days

## Invoice Processing

- Invoices must reference the PO number and match the contracted amounts
- Three-way matching (PO, receipt, invoice) is required for goods purchases
- Invoice disputes must be raised within 10 business days of receipt
- Payment terms default to 60 days end-of-month unless contractually agreed otherwise

## Vendor Performance

- Vendors on contracts exceeding 10M JPY annually must undergo annual performance reviews
- Critical service providers must have business continuity plans on file
- Vendor compliance with contractual SLAs must be monitored quarterly
