# Revenue Recognition Guidelines

Guidelines for revenue recognition in accordance with IFRS 15 / ASBJ Statement No. 29.

## Five-Step Model

1. **Identify the contract** — Agreement with a customer creating enforceable rights and obligations
2. **Identify performance obligations** — Distinct goods or services promised in the contract
3. **Determine the transaction price** — Amount of consideration expected in exchange
4. **Allocate the transaction price** — Distribute to each performance obligation based on standalone selling price
5. **Recognize revenue** — When (or as) the performance obligation is satisfied

## Key Rules

- Revenue must not be recognized before control transfers to the customer
- Multi-element arrangements must be separated into distinct performance obligations
- Variable consideration must be estimated using the expected value or most likely amount method
- Contract modifications must be assessed to determine if a separate contract or modification of existing
- License revenue: right-to-use recognized at point in time; right-to-access recognized over time

## SaaS / Subscription Revenue

- Subscription revenue recognized ratably over the subscription period
- Setup fees assessed for distinct performance obligation; if not distinct, recognized over the subscription term
- Usage-based fees recognized as usage occurs

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Recognizing revenue before delivery of goods or completion of services
- Failing to defer revenue for undelivered elements in bundled contracts
- Recognizing the full contract value upon signing rather than upon delivery
- Ignoring significant financing components in long-term contracts
